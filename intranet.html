TERM 08

Intranet

A private, secure network that uses Internet technologies to share information and resources exclusively within an organisation.

What is an Intranet?

An intranet is a private network accessible only to an organisation's members, employees, or authorised users. It uses the same technologies as the Internet — such as HTTP, HTML, and TCP/IP — but is restricted to internal use only.

Think of it as a private version of the Internet, built specifically for one organisation's internal communication and collaboration needs.

Common Uses of an Intranet

  • Sharing company news, policies, and announcements
  • Hosting internal documents, forms, and manuals
  • Employee directories and internal communication tools
  • Project management and collaboration platforms
  • HR portals for leave applications and payslips

Intranet vs Internet

  • The Internet is public and accessible to anyone — the Intranet is private
  • The Internet spans the globe — an Intranet is limited to one organisation
  • Both use the same underlying web technologies
  • Intranets are protected by firewalls to prevent unauthorised access

Benefits of an Intranet

💡 A well-designed intranet improves internal communication, reduces email overload, centralises important resources, and boosts employee productivity — all within a secure, controlled environment.

Real World Examples

Universities use intranets to give students access to course materials, timetables, and results. Companies use them to manage HR, share policies, and collaborate on projects. Hospitals use intranets to securely share patient records among authorised staff.
