TERM 09

Extranet

A controlled private network that allows limited access to authorised external users such as partners, suppliers, or clients.

What is an Extranet?

An extranet is an extension of an organisation's intranet that is partially accessible to authorised outside users. It sits between a private intranet and the public Internet, allowing selected external parties to access specific internal resources securely.

Access is typically granted through secure login credentials and is protected by firewalls and encryption.

Who Uses an Extranet?

  • Business partners — to collaborate on shared projects
  • Suppliers — to check inventory levels and place orders
  • Clients — to track orders, view reports, or access services
  • Franchisees — to access brand guidelines and operational tools
  • Contractors — to submit work and access project documentation

Extranet vs Intranet vs Internet

  • Internet — open to everyone, no restrictions
  • Intranet — closed network, only internal employees
  • Extranet — controlled access, selected external users only

Benefits of an Extranet

💡 Extranets improve collaboration with external partners, reduce communication delays, streamline supply chains, and enhance customer service — all while maintaining a high level of security and control.

Real World Examples

A bank may give corporate clients access to an extranet portal to manage accounts. A manufacturer may give suppliers access to check production schedules. A university may give partner institutions access to shared research databases.
